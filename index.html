Data Visualization

Did the Ebola outbreak lead to more asylum seekers in West Africa?

Serkan Sönmez, Georg Becker

Reading

This visualization is a line chart that displays trends in asylum seekers in three West African countries Sierra Leone, Liberia, and Guinea over the years 2000 to 2016. The horizontal axis represents the years, while the vertical axis shows the number of asylum seekers. Each line corresponds to a specific country: red for Liberia, beige/green for Sierra Leone, and blue for Guinea. Key events are annotated on the chart to highlight their potential impact on asylum seeker trends, such as the Civil War in Sierra Leone, elections in Guinea, and the West African Ebola epidemic. These events align with noticeable peaks or changes in the trend

Analyzing

By exploring the chart, we observed that the number of asylum seekers from Sierra Leone peaked around 2002 during the Civil War and declined steadily afterward. Guinea saw a dramatic increase in asylum seekers after 2010, coinciding with the re-election of Alpha Condé and further exacerbated by the West African Ebola epidemic. Liberia's numbers remained relatively stable, with minor fluctuations. This analysis highlights how political instability, conflict, and health crises significantly influence asylum-seeking patterns.

Interacting

You can hover over the lines to view precise values and additional details for a specific year. Tooltips display the exact number of asylum seekers for each country and event descriptions at particular points. This interactivity allows for closer inspection of the data at critical moments, such as peaks or transitions.
